Renewable Energy Australia

A visual analysis of Australia's renewable energy usage.

This interactive map visualizes renewable energy consumption patterns
across various states in Australia. The map highlights
the distribution of renewable energy plants , providing insights
into how different types of renewable energy sources,
such as wind, solar, hydro, and bioenergy, contribute
to each state's energy mix.

This bubble chart above illustrates the percentage of energy consumed by different Australian states, categorized by fuel type. Each bubble represents a state, and its size corresponds to the percentage of energy consumption for a specific fuel type.

The line graph shows the total energy production in Australia from 2001 to 2023, segmented by fuel types.

Renewable energy production has significantly increased since 2010, contrasting with a decline in coal and oil production. This reflects the national shift towards cleaner energy sources.

Solar energy generation has experienced rapid growth, especially in recent years, while hydro remains a consistent contributor to Australia’s renewable energy mix.
